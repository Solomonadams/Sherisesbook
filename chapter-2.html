Growth Will Cost You Old Versions Of Yourself

Growth is not gentle. It does not ask for permission, and it rarely feels comfortable.

One of the hardest truths about becoming is this: you will have to outgrow versions of yourself that once kept you safe. The version of you that tolerated too much. The version that stayed quiet to keep the peace. The version that accepted less because she didn’t yet know she deserved more. And letting those versions go will not feel like celebration at first it will feel like loss.

You may miss who you used to be. You may even question if you are becoming too “different” for the people around you. But growth always requires distance from familiarity..

But growth always requires distance from familiarity.

There will be moments where you realize: you don’t react the same way anymore you no longer accept what you once tolerated you cannot return to what you have outgrown And that is not destruction, that is evolution.

Becoming costs comfort. Becoming costs old mindsets. Becoming sometimes costs relationships, silence, and certainty.

But what you gain is deeper: clarity, peace, identity, and strength.

You are not losing yourself. You are meeting the version of you that was buried under survival.

And even though it feels unfamiliar, it is still you, just freer. You’re Rising and that’s all that matters
