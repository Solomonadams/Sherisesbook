About The Author

Halima Sadiya Hadi

Amb. Halima Sadiya Hadi is a Nigerian humanitarian, entrepreneur, author, and youth advocate passionate about empowering women, uplifting communities, and creating meaningful social impact. She is the founder of Halhad Foundation and the author of She Rises, a transformative project created to remind women that they are allowed to heal, rest, grow, and rediscover themselves even through life’s most difficult seasons.

Growing up, Halima experienced the realities of responsibility and adulthood earlier than expected. Navigating life, womanhood, and personal challenges at a young age shaped her understanding of resilience, identity, and the silent struggles many women face behind closed doors. Having to find herself piece by piece through life’s uncertainties became part of the foundation of her story and purpose. Those experiences later inspired She Rises — a movement created for women who are constantly surviving for others while slowly forgetting themselves. Through her writing and advocacy, she hopes to remind women that healing, softness, rest, and transformation are still possible regardless of where life begins.

Halima strongly believes that impact is not measured by how loud a person is, but by the lives they touch. With this belief, she has dedicated herself to supporting vulnerable women and youth through mentorship, humanitarian outreach, health awareness, education, empowerment initiatives, and community development programs. Through Halhad Foundation, she continues to champion causes centered around women empowerment, youth development, dignity, hygiene education, and sustainable support systems for underserved communities.

A graduate of Political Science currently advancing her studies in Business Management, Halima combines leadership, diplomacy, creativity, and advocacy in her approach to nation-building and humanitarian service. Her passion for governance, development, and social responsibility continues to influence both her personal and professional journey. Beyond advocacy, she is deeply passionate about fashion, media, storytelling, and creative arts. She has participated in acting projects and continues to explore creativity as a tool for inspiration, representation, and positive social influence.

Known for her compassion, emotional intelligence, integrity, and resilience, Halima represents a new generation of African women leaders women who lead not only with ambition, but with empathy, purpose, and authenticity.

As the first child in a family of four, she developed a deep sense of responsibility from an early age. Her experiences as both a caregiver and provider taught her the value of strength, patience, sacrifice, and perseverance qualities that continue to shape her vision today. Through every project, initiative, and platform she builds, Halima’s mission remains the same: to create spaces where people feel seen, supported, empowered, and inspired to rise into the fullest version of themselves.
